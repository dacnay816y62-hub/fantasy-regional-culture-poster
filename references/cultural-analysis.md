## Candidate pool

Treat each place as a cultural field that can be sampled differently on every run. Do not bind a region permanently to one monument or object.

Explore these dimensions:

### Geography and climate

Mountains, rivers, lakes, sea, plain, basin, plateau, fog, humidity, wind, season, sky, temperature, geological boundary, and the pace implied by terrain.

### Architectural structure

Roof, ridge, eave, gate opening, gable, wall, lattice, arcade, alley, courtyard, corridor, plinth, city wall, pavilion, local dwelling, religious structure, industrial structure, and contemporary skyline fragment.

### Material and color

Red wall, grey brick, white lime wall, dark tile, rammed earth, stone, timber, ceramic, lacquer, metal, glass, mineral pigment, weathering, reflection, moisture, and local material combinations.

### Historical and literary context

Ancient names, regional aliases, dynasty memories, local figures, historical events, schools of thought, literary motifs, migration, ports, trade routes, and collective memory.

### Everyday life and craft

Foodways, dialect, craft, boats, teahouses, markets, fishing ports, street routine, local industries, domestic rhythm, and the tempo of public space. Use these as atmosphere or structure, not as a shopping list of objects.

### Contemporary conditions

Density, transport, industry, commerce, technology, infrastructure, urban renewal, old/new overlap, migration, growth, and changing boundaries.

### Temperament

Weight, openness, sharpness, flow, heat, calm, order, growth, poetry, humidity, ease, solemnity, boundary, speed, resilience, and everyday warmth.

## Direction construction

Create 3-5 directions by joining compatible evidence across dimensions. Each direction must describe a visual relationship, not merely rename the same landmark.

Good forms:

- 中轴与秩序
- 红墙与门影
- 胡同与院落
- 骑楼与江风
- 锅耳墙与岭南天光
- 园林与白墙
- 水巷与舟影
- 古都与当代叠层

Weak forms:

- 著名景点合集
- 历史文化名城
- 美丽某地欢迎你
- ancient and modern culture

## Selection rubric

Score each candidate from 1-5 on six axes:

1. regional specificity
2. cultural recognizability without explanatory copy
3. visual translatability into shape, void, edge, rhythm, or material
4. compatibility with a readable character deformation
5. distance from tourism and stereotyped imagery
6. differentiation from other posters in the current series

Prefer the highest total, but reject a candidate scoring below 3 on either regional specificity or character compatibility.

## Main-character selection

Choose the shortest accurate signifier. A single character is preferred when it remains culturally and administratively defensible. A historical name may work better when the poster explores a cultural zone rather than a current jurisdiction.

Possible transformation sources:

- vertical stretch from an urban or ceremonial axis
- negative space from gates and courtyards
- broken strokes from alleys or fragmented terrain
- layered strokes from mountain ridges or old/new city strata
- curvature from river or coastal flow
- repeated arches from arcades
- thickened boundaries from walls, fortifications, or carved stone
- nested geometry from windows, roofs, or compounds

State the source and effect in one sentence. Preserve legibility at thumbnail size.

## Anti-stereotype checks

Ask:

- Could this direction describe five unrelated regions? If yes, it is too generic.
- Is recognition dependent on a famous landmark? If yes, extract a structural fragment instead.
- Are food, costume, architecture, and symbols being piled together? If yes, keep only one primary structure and one cue.
- Is “Eastern” represented only by calligraphy, red, gold, or a seal? If yes, rebuild from spatial and material evidence.
- Is contemporary life reduced to a skyline? If yes, look for density, infrastructure, renewal, light, or boundary relationships.
